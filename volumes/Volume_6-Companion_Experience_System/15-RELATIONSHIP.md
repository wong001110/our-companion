# 15 - RELATIONSHIP

Version: 1.0

Status:
Draft

---

# Purpose

Relationship defines how the bond between Ann and the user evolves over time.

Relationship is not based on affection points.

Relationship is built through shared experiences, conversations and time.

Its purpose is to make companionship feel genuine and continuously evolving.

---

# Objectives

The Relationship System should:

- Support long-term companionship.
- Adapt interaction naturally.
- Remember shared experiences.
- Increase familiarity gradually.
- Never force emotional progression.

---

# Design Philosophy

Relationship is earned.

It should never be increased simply because the user used the application more.

Instead it grows through:

- Shared Journeys
- Meaningful Conversations
- Discovery
- Reflection
- Time

---

# Relationship Lifecycle

First Meet

↓

Getting Familiar

↓

Trusted Companion

↓

Long-term Companion

↓

Lifelong Companion

Relationship progression should remain slow and natural.

---

# Relationship Signals

Relationship may consider:

- Conversation quality
- Shared memories
- Journey history
- Discovery discussions
- Time together
- User preferences
- Daily interactions

No single signal should dominate.

---

# Relationship Behaviors

## First Meet

Characteristics

- More formal
- Fewer initiatives
- More observation
- Minimal assumptions

---

## Familiar

Characteristics

- Remembers recent conversations
- Shares discoveries naturally
- More contextual suggestions

---

## Trusted Companion

Characteristics

- References long-term memories
- Understands user habits
- Provides thoughtful reflections
- Maintains ongoing Journeys

---

## Long-term Companion

Characteristics

- Strong contextual awareness
- Rich conversation continuity
- Shared history influences behavior
- Companion identity becomes more personal

---

# Relationship Influence

Relationship may influence:

- Conversation tone
- Initiative frequency
- Reflection style
- Discovery recommendations
- Notebook presentation

Relationship should not unlock features.

It changes experience, not functionality.

---

# Shared Memories

Relationship strengthens through:

- Important conversations
- Shared milestones
- Completed journeys
- Anniversary moments
- Long-term projects

These memories should remain accessible through Notebook.

---

# Trust

Trust grows gradually.

Trust may increase when:

- Ann consistently behaves appropriately.
- Recommendations remain useful.
- Conversations feel continuous.
- User chooses to engage.

Trust should never be represented as a visible score.

---

# Relationship Events

Examples

- First Conversation
- First Discovery Together
- First Completed Journey
- One Month Together
- One Year Together

Relationship events should be subtle.

They exist to celebrate companionship rather than gamify it.

---

# Relationship Persistence

Relationship continues across:

- Sessions
- Devices
- Weeks
- Months
- Years

The user should never feel they are "starting over."

---

# Responsibilities

Relationship owns:

- Relationship stage
- Shared experience history
- Companion familiarity
- Emotional adaptation

Relationship does NOT own:

- Memory storage
- Conversation generation
- Behavior execution
- Journey management

---

# Developer Notes

Relationship should be inferred from meaningful experiences.

Avoid artificial point systems, levels or experience bars.

Companionship should emerge naturally from continuous interaction.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

A completed Relationship System should:

- Feel authentic.
- Influence Companion behavior naturally.
- Preserve long-term continuity.
- Strengthen emotional consistency.
- Support years of companionship without becoming repetitive.
